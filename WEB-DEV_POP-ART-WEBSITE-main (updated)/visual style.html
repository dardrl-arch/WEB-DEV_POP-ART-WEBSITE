ON WEDNESDAYS,

The 2000s graphic scene was all about controlled chaos
—a mix of structure and rebellion that defined the digital age.

Designers began playing with curves and asymmetry,
breaking traditional layouts to mirror the energy of pop culture
and the internet boom.

Web pages, CD covers, and magazine spreads experimented
with layers, visual tilts, compositions, and floating elements giving
designs a sense of movement and personality.
