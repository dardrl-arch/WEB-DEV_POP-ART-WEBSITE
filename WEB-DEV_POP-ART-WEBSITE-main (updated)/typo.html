TYPOGRAPHY
AND COLORS

“These colors don't just pop—they bop like
it's 2002.”

The 2000s were all about
loud expression

Bold, flashy, and unapologetically fun. Typography went maximalist: bubble fonts, chrome effects,
pixel text, and graffiti-inspired lettering mirrored the era’s obsession with individuality
and tech futurism.

At the same time, color palettes exploded—metallic silvers, neon pinks, icy blues,
and holographic gradients reflected the digital age’s optimism
and the rise of consumer tech.
